Sandra Young, B.A.

Executive Chair, Atisundara Foundation

Sandra Young is the Executive Chair of the Atisundara Foundation, overseeing the Foundation’s work to promote early childhood education in Guyana and among First Nations in Canada. In this role, she draws on her experience in social services, such as her work at the Toronto Board of Education and as the founder of The Ashanti Room, a private sector retailer of Afro-centric children's products.

Expertise


Sandra oversees funding to the Foundation’s activities in Canada and Guyana with a particular emphasis on early childhood education as a cornerstone of long-term economic growth that is inclusive of a broad cross-section of society.

Early childhood education (ECE) refers to programs for children up to 8 years old based on an explicit curriculum delivered by qualified staff and designed to support children's development and learning. Settings may include child care centres, nursery schools, preschools, pre- or junior kindergarten, kindergarten and play school.

Sandra was a co-founder of an innovative private sector retailer, the Ashanti Room. The Ashanti Room was a forerunner of the now more mainstream practice of consciously including African representation in children’s play and learning material. In addition to providing Afro-centric home furnishing and other products specifically designed to be inclusive of African culture such as paintings and other décor items, the store sold Afro-centric books, cards, dolls and other toys as well as health items such as shea butter.

Sandra's work and dedication to peace building though insight meditation has been recognized by the Governor of Chiangmai Province, Thailand who awarded her the benefactor of Buddhism award in 2018. Currently, Sandra is co-founder and pro-bono teacher in residence at the Buddhavipassana Meditation Centre in Toronto.
